# Healthcare wage-grade map (dirug map)

Israeli public-healthcare pay is set by public-sector collective agreements
negotiated by the Histadrut and the profession unions with the Wage Commissioner
(haMemune al haSachar) at the Finance Ministry. Each occupation sits in a wage
grade (dirug); the base salary is a combined-salary cell of grade (daraga) by
seniority (vetek) read from that grade's table. This file maps who falls under
which grade, so the reader picks the right table before reading any number.

## The grades

| Worker | Dirug (wage grade) | Notes |
|--------|--------------------|-------|
| Certified and practical nurses | Nurses' dirug (dirug achim ve'achayot) | One grade for the nursing profession; represented by the nurses' union within the Histadrut. |
| Occupational therapists | Occupational-therapy dirug (dirug ripui be'isuk) | One of three separate allied-health tracks. |
| Physiotherapists | Physiotherapists' dirug | Separate allied-health track. |
| Dietitians and speech clinicians | Para-medical dirug (dirug para-refui) | The para-medical grade covers dietitians and speech clinicians. |
| Hospital pharmacists, medical physicists, some lab scientists | Academic dirug (dirug haMachar) | These are NOT in a health dirug; they sit in the general academic grade. Confirm the exact track from the current table. |
| Doctors | Doctors' dirug under the IMA (haRi) agreement | A distinct agreement; pay is dominated by on-call and duty, which are not base salary. |

## Why the grade matters

- The base combined-salary cell comes only from the correct grade's table. The
  wrong grade gives the wrong base.
- The tosafot differ by grade: the nurses' tosefet achayot is a nurses'-dirug
  line; tosefet hachsharot banded 3.50% / 9.00% / 9.50% is an allied-health
  line; on-call day-equivalents are a doctors' line.
- Allied health is three grades, not one. Do not carry a figure from occupational
  therapy to physiotherapy or to the para-medical grade.
- Two workers on the same ward can be on different grades (a nurse in the nurses'
  dirug, a pharmacist in the academic dirug), so their slips are not comparable
  line for line.

## Employer of record (separate from the grade)

The same grade is paid by different employers: the Ministry of Health
(government hospitals), Clalit (which owns hospitals and community clinics),
Hadassah, or a municipal hospital. The employer issues the payslip, can run a
different pension arrangement, and can add employer-specific supplements (for
example a management supplement at Clalit), even though the underlying grade
table is the same. Establish the employer before reading a slip.
