# Reform hour structure: Ofek Chadash vs Oz LaTmura

Every teacher salary starts from the reform's work-week definition. Get the
reform and the school level right first, because the number of frontal hours,
private hours, and the total position all change between them.

## Two hour concepts you must not mix

- **Frontal hour (shaat horaa frontalit / shaot pruntaliyot)**: standing in
  front of a class (also role hours like homeroom or Bagrut prep in Oz).
- **Private hour (shaa pratanit)**: teaching a single student or a small group.
  Ofek: up to 5 students. Oz: up to 3 students, or up to 5 by a pedagogical-team
  decision.
- **Stay / support hour (shaat shehiya in Ofek, shaa tomechet horaa in Oz)**:
  time at school for meetings, prep, parent talks, and checking work. Paid, but
  it is NOT front-of-class teaching.

The full weekly position is the SUM of all three. A common error is to treat
only the frontal hours as "the job".

## Ofek Chadash (kindergarten + elementary + junior-high)

Full 100% position = 36 weekly hours.

| Level | Frontal | Private | Stay | Total |
|-------|---------|---------|------|-------|
| Elementary (yesodi) | 26 | 5 | 5 | 36 |
| Junior-high (chativat beynayim) | 23 | 4 | 9 | 36 |

Kindergarten (gan) teachers are also under Ofek Chadash: the reform runs in
kindergartens, elementary schools, and middle schools. The gan work-week is
defined separately from the two school rows above, so read the current gan
structure from the union or the ministry rather than reusing an elementary row.

Source: education.gov.il Portal Ovdei Horaa work-week page; union structure PDF;
mosdot.education.gov.il Ofek reform page and pob.education.gov.il kindergarten
Ofek page for the kindergarten coverage.

## Oz LaTmura (upper-secondary only)

Base position historically = 40 weekly hours.

| Component | Hours | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Frontal | 24 | Includes role hours (homeroom, Bagrut prep) |
| Private | 6 | Group up to 3 (up to 5 by pedagogical decision) |
| Teaching-support | 10 | See breakdown below |
| **Total** | **40** | |

Ten support hours: work meetings 2/wk, team training 2/wk, parent talks 1/wk,
prep and checking work and exams 5/wk.

**Change from tashpe (school year 2024/25):** the full position becomes 38
weekly hours, with 25 frontal and 3 private hours (the 10 support hours stay).
Older payslips and older tables still show the 40-hour base, so check the year
before you read a table. (The 38-hour split is from a payroll-provider circular;
confirm the exact current split against the union table or the official
calculator for the specific school year.)

## Practical implication

Two teachers with the same rank and seniority, one in Ofek elementary and one in
Oz upper-secondary, sit on DIFFERENT base-pay tables and have different position
sizes. Never carry a number from one reform to the other. When someone says "I
teach 24 hours", ask which reform and which hours (frontal only, or the full
position), because 24 frontal in Oz is a full position while 24 in Ofek is not.
